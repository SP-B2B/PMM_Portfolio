RyzeUp: Product Naming, Brand Development & Go-to-Market Foundation

Helping launch an employee engagement platform from an unnamed product into a market-ready SaaS brand.

↓ Download PDF
PMM Competencies Demonstrated
Product Naming Brand Development Messaging Strategy Website Strategy Product Communications Go-to-Market Support Customer Messaging Launch Communications Cross-Functional Collaboration
Situation

RyzeUp is a workplace motivation and performance gamification platform designed to help organizations improve employee engagement, productivity, and performance through goal-oriented challenges and reward systems.

When the client engaged Blufig, the product already existed, but there was no market-facing brand, website, or messaging foundation. The company needed a clear identity and a digital presence that could communicate the product's value to prospective customers.

As one of the earliest members assigned to the project, I became heavily involved in shaping how the product would be introduced to the market.

Challenge

The platform faced several launch challenges:

  • No product name or market-facing identity
  • No website or digital presence
  • No defined brand voice or messaging framework
  • Complex product benefits that needed to be translated into simple business outcomes
  • Need to establish credibility and awareness in a competitive employee engagement market

The challenge was to transform a functional product into a recognizable SaaS brand with a clear story, messaging foundation, and launch-ready digital presence.

Approach

I worked closely with internal stakeholders throughout the branding and launch process, contributing to the product's identity, messaging, and customer-facing communications.

My involvement spanned naming, brand development, website strategy, messaging, and launch support.

I researched how employee engagement and gamification platforms positioned their products, analyzed messaging patterns across competitor websites, and used those insights to help shape RyzeUp's communication strategy.

Responsibilities & Deliverables
Product Naming & Brand Development
  • Participated in product naming workshops and contributed several options out of which this one got selected.
  • Contributed to brand identity discussions including tagline development, brand voice, and overall messaging direction.
Website Strategy & Messaging
  • Developed end-to-end website content and launch messaging.
  • Contributed to website structure discussions and helped shape how product information would be organized and presented to prospective customers.
  • Created messaging focused on translating platform capabilities into business outcomes relevant to organizational performance and employee engagement.
Launch Communications
  • Supported website launch initiatives and helped establish the company's initial digital presence.
  • Developed ongoing social media and blog content to support awareness, education, and brand visibility.
Outcomes

The initiative resulted in:

  • Launch of the RyzeUp brand and digital presence
  • Establishment of the product's first market-facing identity
  • Creation of a complete website and customer-facing messaging foundation
  • Consistent brand voice across website and social channels
  • Successful transition from product concept to market-ready SaaS brand
  • Continued content and social media initiatives supporting early-stage market awareness and customer engagement
Why This Project Matters

This project represented my first experience contributing to the launch of a B2B SaaS product from the ground up.

It was the first time I participated in product naming, brand development, website strategy, and launch communications within a SaaS environment. The experience provided early exposure to the intersection of product, marketing, branding, and customer communication that later shaped my work in product communications and product marketing.
