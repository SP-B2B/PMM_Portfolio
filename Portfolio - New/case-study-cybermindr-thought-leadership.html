Building CyberMindr's Thought Leadership & Market Education Function

Establishing CyberMindr's market voice, educational content ecosystem, and thought leadership program from the ground up.

↓ Download PDF
PMM Competencies Demonstrated
Thought Leadership Strategy Market Education Product Marketing Technical Marketing Sales Enablement Executive Communications Content Operations Webinar & Event Marketing Cross-Functional Collaboration Go-to-Market Support
Situation

When I joined WATI, CyberMindr did not yet exist as an independent brand. The platform operated as i-Radar, a cybersecurity product within WATI, with no dedicated content function, thought leadership program, educational resources, or market-facing content strategy.

As CyberMindr evolved into a standalone cybersecurity company, leadership recognized the need to establish market awareness, educate buyers, support sales conversations, and build credibility within emerging cybersecurity categories such as CTEM, Exposure Management, and Third-Party Risk Management.

The challenge was not simply creating content.

The challenge was building CyberMindr's market education and thought leadership capability from the ground up.

Challenge

Several foundational gaps existed:

  • No content strategy
  • No editorial framework
  • No educational content ecosystem
  • No whitepaper program
  • No webinar program
  • No SEO strategy
  • No established thought leadership presence
  • Limited resources and a single-person content function

At the same time, the company needed to establish credibility in highly competitive cybersecurity markets while supporting both brand awareness and enterprise sales initiatives.

Approach

Rather than treating content as a standalone marketing activity, I built a market education strategy aligned with CyberMindr's positioning, buyer personas, and go-to-market objectives.

I developed the systems, processes, and content programs necessary to consistently educate buyers, support sales conversations, and reinforce CyberMindr's market narrative.

Content priorities were informed by:

  • Product roadmap discussions
  • Sales team feedback
  • Competitor analysis
  • Industry research
  • Analyst reports
  • Search demand and buyer intent

This ensured educational content directly supported both customer needs and business objectives.

Responsibilities & Deliverables
Content Strategy & Editorial Operations
  • Developed CyberMindr's content strategy, editorial calendar, content pillars, topic clusters, and content planning processes.
  • Established repeatable workflows, review checklists, and content development systems to support long-term scalability.
Thought Leadership Program
  • Authored more than 10 enterprise cybersecurity whitepapers focused on CTEM, Exposure Management, Third-Party Risk Management, and industry-specific cybersecurity challenges.
  • Ghostwrote executive thought leadership content and founder communications supporting market visibility and industry credibility.
  • Developed eBooks, educational resources, and long-form content designed to support buyer education.
Market Education & Sales Enablement
  • Created blogs, whitepapers, sales collateral, brochures, email nurture assets, and customer-facing educational content used throughout the buyer journey.
  • Supported sales teams by developing content assets used in prospect conversations, event follow-ups, and customer engagement initiatives.
Events & Industry Engagement
  • Planned and supported webinars, industry events, and conferences through landing pages, promotional campaigns, event collateral, speaker communications, moderator notes, and attendee engagement programs.
  • Hosted CyberMindr's first webinar, managing planning, registration workflows, attendee communications, event execution, and post-event follow-up.
  • Coordinated CyberMindr's participation in industry conferences and events, managing event communications, booth assets, vendor coordination, and supporting logistics.
Outcomes

The initiative resulted in:

  • Creation of CyberMindr's first dedicated content and thought leadership function
  • Establishment of a scalable market education program supporting long-term growth
  • Development of a cybersecurity content library spanning thought leadership, educational resources, whitepapers, webinars, and customer-facing assets
  • Increased utilization of educational content across sales conversations and customer engagement initiatives
  • Stronger market awareness and category visibility across CTEM, Exposure Management, and cybersecurity risk management discussions
  • Increased analyst visibility and industry recognition
  • Consistent brand voice across educational, marketing, sales, and executive communications
  • Alignment between Product, Marketing, Leadership, and Sales teams through shared messaging and educational content initiatives
Why This Project Matters

This project was less about content production and more about building organizational capability.

Starting with no established content infrastructure, I created the systems, processes, educational programs, and thought leadership initiatives that enabled CyberMindr to consistently communicate its expertise, educate buyers, and participate in industry conversations.

The experience demonstrated how market education, thought leadership, and customer communication can become strategic assets that support positioning, awareness, and go-to-market execution.
