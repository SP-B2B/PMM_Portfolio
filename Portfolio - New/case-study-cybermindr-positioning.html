Situation

When I joined, CyberMindr existed as i-Radar, a cybersecurity platform operating under WATI. The product specialized in identifying and validating attack paths, vulnerabilities, and security exposures using MITRE ATT&CK-aligned methodologies.

While the platform had strong technical capabilities, its market narrative lacked clarity. Messaging was highly technical, inconsistent across channels, and failed to clearly communicate differentiation or business value.

As the product evolved into CyberMindr, there was an opportunity to establish a stronger market position and build a scalable messaging foundation to support growth, analyst engagement, and go-to-market initiatives.

Challenge

The platform faced several go-to-market challenges:

  • No clearly defined market category or narrative
  • Limited differentiation from Exposure Management and Attack Surface Management competitors
  • Messaging that resonated with technical audiences but not business stakeholders
  • No website or unified messaging architecture
  • Lack of buyer personas and audience-specific messaging
  • No standardized framework for sales, marketing, and executive communications

At the same time, the cybersecurity market was shifting toward Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM), creating an opportunity to align the product with emerging buyer priorities.

Strategic Shift

Rather than positioning CyberMindr primarily as a vulnerability discovery platform, we shifted the narrative toward exposure validation, risk-based prioritization, remediation verification, attacker-perspective security assessments, and continuous exposure discovery.

This enabled clearer differentiation from traditional attack surface management and vulnerability assessment vendors while creating a stronger connection to executive-level cybersecurity priorities.

As CyberMindr transitioned from a product operating under WATI into an independent cybersecurity platform, this messaging framework became the foundation for the new brand's market identity, website narrative, and customer-facing communications.

Approach

I conducted competitive and market analysis to evaluate how CTEM and Exposure Management vendors were positioning their solutions. This included reviewing messaging, category narratives, and differentiation strategies across vendors such as Pentera, CyCognito, Tenable, and FireCompass.

Based on these insights, I helped reposition CyberMindr around exposure validation, risk-based prioritization, remediation verification, attacker-perspective security assessments, and continuous exposure discovery.

Working closely with Product, Leadership, and technical stakeholders, I led the development of CyberMindr's first formal positioning and messaging framework, translating complex technical capabilities into clear business outcomes and creating a consistent narrative across customer-facing channels.

Responsibilities & Deliverables
Positioning & Messaging
  • Defined CyberMindr's positioning, category narrative, and competitive differentiation within the CTEM and Exposure Management market.
  • Developed the company's first formal messaging framework, including buyer personas, audience-specific value propositions, and messaging for CISOs, MSSPs, Security Analysts, and Risk Management teams.
  • Conducted competitive analysis to identify market whitespace and strengthen differentiation against emerging cybersecurity vendors.
Website & Customer Experience
  • Led website strategy, narrative development, information architecture, and messaging across homepage, product, solution, and industry pages.
  • Built a scalable messaging foundation that aligned customer-facing communications across marketing, sales, and product functions.
Go-to-Market & Enablement
  • Created thought leadership content, whitepapers, PR assets, executive communications, campaign messaging, and sales enablement materials.
  • Planned and supported webinars, industry events, and conferences, developing event messaging, content, and supporting collateral to drive awareness and engagement.
Growth & Market Visibility
  • Developed and executed content and SEO initiatives that generated 144,000+ Google search impressions, 3,500+ organic clicks, and nearly 8,000 organic visitors.
  • Authored a high-performing thought leadership article that attracted 1,400+ new users and became one of the company's top-performing landing pages.
  • Partnered cross-functionally with Product, Marketing, Sales, Leadership, and technical teams to establish a consistent market narrative across customer touchpoints.
Outcomes

The initiative contributed to:

  • Two major website redesigns and narrative transformations
  • CyberMindr's first structured positioning and messaging framework
  • Consistent messaging across Product, Marketing, Sales, and Leadership teams
  • Stronger differentiation within CTEM and Exposure Management markets
  • Improved customer understanding, sales conversations, and internal alignment
  • Increased analyst visibility and market awareness through thought leadership, campaigns, events, PR initiatives, and industry engagement
  • A 300% increase in organic website traffic through content, SEO, and website messaging initiatives
  • Organic acquisition contributing approximately 26% of total website traffic
  • More than 144,000 Google search impressions and 3,500 organic clicks generated through website and content initiatives
