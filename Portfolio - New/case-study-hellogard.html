HelloGard: Repositioning a Robotics Company into a SaaS Platform Narrative

Transforming market perception from robot reseller to software-driven fleet intelligence platform.

Situation

HelloGard operated in the robotics and automation space, offering both robotic solutions and a proprietary software platform designed to manage, monitor, and optimize autonomous robot fleets.

While the company had developed a powerful fleet management platform capable of tracking robot performance, utilization, movement, and operational efficiency across facilities, its website and customer-facing messaging primarily positioned the business as a robot reseller.

As a result, the company's most valuable differentiator, its software platform, was being overshadowed by its hardware partnerships.

The business needed to shift market perception from "a company that sells robots" to "a company that provides the intelligence layer powering robotic operations."

Challenge

Several positioning challenges limited the company's ability to communicate its value effectively:

  • Existing messaging focused heavily on hardware rather than software
  • The proprietary fleet management platform was not present across customer-facing communications
  • The website lacked a clear SaaS narrative and failed to communicate long-term business value
  • Product differentiation was unclear despite the platform's vendor-agnostic architecture
  • Prospective customers could easily perceive HelloGard as a robotics reseller rather than a software company

The challenge was to reposition the company around its software platform while maintaining the credibility and partnerships established through its robotics ecosystem.

Strategic Shift

The core positioning challenge was moving the narrative from:

"We sell robots."

to

"We help enterprises manage, optimize, and scale autonomous robotic operations through a centralized fleet intelligence platform."

Rather than emphasizing individual robotic products, the new narrative focused on operational visibility, automation, utilization, efficiency, and software-driven decision making.

A key element of this repositioning was highlighting the platform's vendor-agnostic architecture, enabling organizations to manage existing robotic fleets while maintaining flexibility as their automation strategy evolved.

Approach

I conducted research into how robotics, automation, and technology companies positioned software-driven solutions within traditionally hardware-focused industries.

This included evaluating messaging approaches used by companies that successfully positioned software as the primary value driver within broader operational ecosystems.

Working closely with stakeholders, I led the messaging direction for the website transformation, helping redefine how the company communicated its value proposition, software capabilities, and market positioning.

Responsibilities & Deliverables
Positioning & Messaging
  • Developed a software-first positioning strategy centered on fleet intelligence, operational visibility, automation, and utilization optimization.
  • Refined core messaging, value propositions, and customer-facing narratives to emphasize software capabilities over hardware offerings.
  • Created messaging that communicated the benefits of vendor-agnostic fleet management and long-term operational flexibility.
Website Strategy
  • Led messaging development across homepage, product pages, solution pages, company pages, and customer-facing website content.
  • Contributed to website architecture discussions to ensure the software platform became the primary focus of the customer journey.
  • Developed website narratives that translated technical platform capabilities into measurable business outcomes.
Customer & Market Communications
  • Created customer-facing messaging supporting marketing, sales, and business development initiatives.
  • Developed targeted communications aligned with customer priorities including efficiency, automation, utilization, and operational visibility.
  • Supported account-based marketing initiatives through audience-specific messaging and campaign content.
Outcomes

The initiative resulted in:

  • Successful repositioning of HelloGard's market narrative around its software platform
  • Website transformation emphasizing SaaS capabilities rather than hardware partnerships
  • Stronger differentiation through vendor-agnostic fleet management positioning
  • Improved alignment between product capabilities and customer-facing communications
  • Consistent messaging across website and marketing channels
  • Internal alignment around a software-first value proposition and market identity
  • Adoption of the new positioning across customer-facing materials and digital experiences
Why This Project Matters

This was my first large-scale repositioning project and one of the opportunities where I led messaging direction rather than simply executing content requests.

The project required to change the market perceptions with a new strategic narrative while helping stakeholders communicate a fundamentally different view of their business.

It reinforced the importance of positioning, messaging, and narrative development in shaping how customers understand and evaluate products.
